Mr Gorman's HTML Guide

A basic HTML Page

Every page is supposed to start and stop with a <html> tag.

The <head> section is where the page title is set and any special scripts are loaded.
The <body> section is where the content of the webpage goes.

<html>

 <head>
  <title>website title</title>
 </head>
 
 <body>
  content of website ...
  blah blah blah...
 </body>
 
</html>

Common Tags

Headings

There are 6 levels of headings with 1 the largest and 6 the smallest.

<h1>Biggest heading</h1>
<h3>Medium heading</h3>
<h6>Smallest heading</h6>

Paragraphs

This organises paragraphs of text

<p>This is in a paragraph</p>

Centering text and images

This puts text in the centre of the screen. It also works on images.
Be careful with the spelling, its the American spelling center not the British spelling centre.

<center>This text will be in the middle</center>

Horizontal Rule

The hr tag makes a ruler mark across the page. Its used to split a page into sections.

<hr>

There's an example of hr below this line.

New line

The BR tag makes a line break. It forces the page to start a new line.

<br>

Formatting Tags

Bold

The b tag makes text bold

<b>This text will be bold</b>

Italic

The i tag makes text italic

<i>This text will be italic</i>

Strikethrough

This creates strikethrough text (text with a line through it) like this.

<strike>This will be crossed out</strike>

Font

This lets you change the font and the colour.

Common Mistakes

Look carefully at how colour is spelt. Its spelled the American way color.
Also look at how the tag is closed. Its just </font>

To change the font:

<font face ="Arial">
Everything inside the tag will be in the Arial font
</font>

To change the colour:

<font color="red">
This will be red
</font>

To do both at the same time:

<font face ="Arial" color="red">
Everything inside the tag will be in the Arial font and red
</font>

To choose an exact shade of a colour you can use a HTML colour code.
These 6 digit codes start with the # symbol eg #7B3AB1.

<font color="#7B3AB1">
This will be the exact shade of purple that I have chosen.
</font>

Hyperlinks

The <a> tag makes a hyperlink. But to make it work you have to add other attributes (things inside the <a> tag).

A basic hyperlink

You need to tell it two things. Where to link to and what to click on to activate the link.

<a href="http://www.westleighhigh.co.uk"> This text will be a link </a>

Here's what it looks like. Try clicking on it: This text will be a link

Common Mistakes

Look carefully at the example. There's a space between a and href.
The page we're linking to is inside speech marks.

An email hyperlink

You can make a hyperlink that emails someone when you click on it.
Its like a normal hyperlink expect that in the speech marks you put mailto: and then an email address.

<a href="mailto:bengorman@westleigh.wigan.sch.uk"> This is what you click on </a>

Lists

There are two types of list. Ordered and unordered lists.

Unordered lists

These are lists with bullet points.

<ul>
    <li>Item 1</li>
    <li>Item 2</li>
    <li>Another item</li>
</ul>

This would display:

  • Item 1
  • Item 2
  • Another item

Common Mistakes

The tag starts and ends with <ul> and </ul>
But every bullet point starts and ends with <li> </li>

Ordered lists

These are lists with numbered bullet points.

<ol>
    <li>Item 1</li>
    <li>Item 2</li>
    <li>Another item</li>
</ol>

This would display:

  1. Item 1
  2. Item 2
  3. Another item

Common Mistakes

The tag starts and ends with <ol> and </ol>
But every bullet point starts and ends with <li> </li>

Tables

Tables can be tricky to understand.
<table> and </table> start and stop the table.
<tr> and </tr> means a new row.
<th> and </th> means the headings. We only use these in the first row.
<td> and </td> means the actual information in that cell of the table.

<table>
    <tr>
        <th>Name</th>
        <th>Form</th>
        <th>Shoe size</th>
    </tr>
    
    <tr>
        <td>Ben Bloggs</td>
        <td>8 O</td>
        <td>8</td>
    </tr>
    
    <tr>
        <td>Suzie Smith</td>
        <td>9 P</td>
        <td>7</td>
    </tr>
    
    <tr>
        <td>Jeff Jeffson</td>
        <td>11 C</td>
        <td>10</td>
    </tr>    
</table>

This would display:

Name Form Shoe size
Ben Bloggs 8 O 8
Suzie Smith 9 P 7
Jeff Jeffson 11 C 10
